Trajectories of plasma biomarkers, amyloid-beta burden and cognitive decline in Alzheimer’s disease: A Longitudinal ADNI Study

Yakoub Y, Qiu T, Peyrot C, Salvadó G, Villeneuve S, Pichet Binette A, ADNI (2025). medRxiv Preprint.

This study demonstrates that it is the rate of change in plasma p-tau217, rather than its baseline level, that best predicts the progression of Alzheimer's disease, highlighting its potential as a dynamic marker for monitoring treatment response and Alzheimer's disease progression.

Plasma p-tau markers and vascular factors are associated with cognitive decline and clinical progression in the CIMA-Q cohort

Cottez R, Peyrot C, Denis HL, Tremblay C, Loiselle A, Filali A, Hébert S, Leclerc N, Zetterber H, Kergoat MJ, Consortium for the Early Identification of Alzheimer’s Disease – Quebec (CIMA-Q), Calon F, Pichet Binette A (2025). medRxiv Preprint

This study shows that plasma p-tau217 protein is the most sensitive biomarker of Alzheimer's disease-related decline, while high blood pressure is a predictor of early cognitive decline, highlighting the importance of multimodal profiling to improve prediction and intervention throughout the AD continuum.

The PREVENT-AD cohort: Accelerating Alzheimer's disease research and treatment in Canada and beyond

Villeneuve S, Poirier J, Breitner JCS, Tremblay-Mercier J, ..., Baillet S, Evans AC, Collins DL, Chakravarty MM, Blennow K, Zetterberg H, Spreng RN, Pichet Binette A; PREVENT‐AD Research Group (2025). Alzheimer's & Dementia.

The PREVENT-AD study is a 12-year longitudinal cohort of cognitively normal older adults at risk for Alzheimer’s disease, now sharing an expanded multimodal dataset, including cognitive, behavioral, plasma, PET, MEG, and MRI measures to advance global research on the presymptomatic stages and progression of AD.

Evaluation of the Revised Criteria for Biological and Clinical Staging of Alzheimer Disease

Pichet Binette A, Smith R, Salvadó G, Tideman P, Glans I, van Westen D, Groot C, Ossenkoppele R, Stomrud E, Parchi P, Zetterberg H, Blennow K, Mattsson-Carlgren N, Janelidze S, Palmqvist S, Hansson O, & ADNI (2025). JAMA Neurology

This study shows that individuals with clinical impairment greater than their biological stage of Alzheimer's disease often have additional pathologies, such as α-synuclein, TDP-43-related atrophy, and small vessel disease, highlighting the crucial role of co-pathologies in symptom severity and the need to evaluate non-AD-related biomarkers for accurate diagnosis and prognosis.
